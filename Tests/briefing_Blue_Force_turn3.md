The turn opens with both sides suffering from poor fortune. Simultaneously, Hammer Battery at B9 attempts its preparatory fires on the F4-G4 corridor, but Red's comms disruption (friction event 1) and poor fortune (roll: 14) mean the fire mission is delayed and the rounds arrive ragged — Shadow Recon advancing along the highway through G4-F4 catches the tail end of the barrage, taking light casualties and momentarily halting before pressing on. Alpha Company, advancing through G5, is near the fringe of the impact zone but largely avoids the worst of it due to the degraded accuracy.

Despite these complications, Blue's movement benefits significantly from the firmer-than-expected ground (friction event 2). The Blue advance is roughly on schedule despite the artillery harassment and fire mission delay. Shadow Recon at F4 now observes Viper Recon still at E4 — but Shadow is a recon unit, not infantry or armor, so Viper's withdrawal trigger has not been met per Red's standing orders. Viper holds, reporting Shadow's arrival at F4 to Red HQ, though the comms disruption causes a delay in that report.

By end of turn, Blue has advanced its entire line approximately 2km westward. The highway corridor is now contested between F4 (Shadow) and E4 (Viper), with the bridge at D5 approximately 1-2km beyond Viper's position. Red's defensive line west of the river remains intact and undisturbed. Thunder Battery has expended supply on a marginally effective fire mission against Viper; Hammer Battery's harassment caused minor friction to Blue's advance but did not halt or significantly attrite it. The bridge at D5 stands intact. Both sides have absorbed minor friction but remain combat-effective.

The turn opens with Viper Recon executing its withdrawal from E4 at first light. The wheeled recon company moves quickly down the highway, crosses the bridge at D5, and reaches C4 without incident — Red's fortune (91) means the withdrawal is textbook, with Viper's commander timing the move precisely and reporting a clean 'all clear' to Hammer Battery. However, Red's comms disruption (friction event 1) introduces a delay: Hammer Battery receives the 'all clear' late, and the fire mission on E4/E5 launches roughly 45-60 minutes behind schedule. Despite this, Red's good fortune means the battery commander has pre-registered the target and fires with confidence when the order finally arrives. Hammer's suppression of E4/E5 is accurate and sustained.

Blue Force's catastrophic fortune (roll: 5) manifests as a single, specific event on the main axis: as Shadow Recon begins to move out of F4 toward E5, a lead vehicle suffers a catastrophic mechanical failure before the column fully departs F4. Shadow remains in F4, unable to advance. The breakdown blocks the highway at F4, creating a chokepoint for Steel Armor advancing behind. Steel reaches G4 cleanly but is slowed and disordered approaching F4 — forward elements push into F4 around the stalled vehicle, but Steel ends the turn at F4 in a disordered state, having consumed most of its movement allowance. The conditional advance to E4 does not occur. Hammer Battery's delayed fires land on E4 and E5 — the vacated ground — with no Blue units present in those hexes. Shadow at F4 is at the eastern edge of the suppression zone but not directly in it; the fires cause disruption and keep Shadow pinned at F4 but do not inflict direct casualties.

Thunder Battery's fire mission against Red Guard at C5 is assessed independently of the highway fortune event. The battery executes its mission from L7 against the light urban target at C5. Range (~10km) and entrenchment (30%) degrade effectiveness, and without a confirmed forward observer at or near C5, the battery relies on map registration. The mission achieves moderate suppression — Red Guard is disrupted and cannot improve its entrenchment this turn, but is not significantly attrited. Red Guard holds its positions. Alpha Company advances cleanly to F5 (Ashbury) on a parallel axis unaffected by the F4 congestion. Bravo Company advances to G6 on the southern axis without incident. By end of turn, Blue's main axis is stalled at F4/G4 (Steel disordered, Shadow broken down), with Alpha at F5 as the westernmost functional Blue element. The bridge at D5 remains intact. Red's defensive line is fully intact with Viper now repositioned at C4 covering the northern approaches.
