## Recent History

### Turn 2
The turn opens with Viper Recon executing its withdrawal from E4 at first light. However, Red's comms disruption (friction event 1) introduces a delay: Hammer Battery receives the 'all clear' late, and the fire mission on E4/E5 launches roughly 45-60 minutes behind schedule. Despite this, Red's good fortune means the battery commander has pre-registered the target and fires with confidence when the order finally arrives. Hammer's suppression of E4/E5 is accurate and sustained.

Blue Force's catastrophic fortune (roll: 5) manifests as a single, specific event on the main axis: as Shadow Recon begins to move out of F4 toward E5, a lead vehicle suffers a catastrophic mechanical failure before the column fully departs F4. Shadow remains in F4, unable to advance. The breakdown blocks the highway at F4, creating a chokepoint for Steel Armor advancing behind. Steel reaches G4 cleanly but is slowed and disordered approaching F4 — forward elements push into F4 around the stalled vehicle, but Steel ends the turn at F4 in a disordered state, having consumed most of its movement allowance. The conditional advance to E4 does not occur. Hammer Battery's delayed fires land on E4 and E5 — the vacated ground — with no Blue units present in those hexes. Shadow at F4 is at the eastern edge of the suppression zone but not directly in it; the fires cause disruption and keep Shadow pinned at F4 but do not inflict direct casualties.

Thunder Battery's fire mission against Red Guard at C5 is assessed independently of the highway fortune event. The battery executes its mission from L7 against the light urban target at C5. Range (~10km) and entrenchment (30%) degrade effectiveness, and without a confirmed forward observer at or near C5, the battery relies on map registration. The mission achieves moderate suppression — Red Guard is disrupted and cannot improve its entrenchment this turn, but is not significantly attrited. Red Guard holds its positions. Alpha Company advances cleanly to F5 (Ashbury) on a parallel axis unaffected by the F4 congestion. Bravo Company advances to G6 on the southern axis without incident. By end of turn, Blue's main axis is stalled at F4/G4 (Steel disordered, Shadow broken down), with Alpha at F5 as the westernmost functional Blue element. The bridge at D5 remains intact. Red's defensive line is fully intact with Viper now repositioned at C4 covering the northern approaches.

### Turn 3
The fog-shrouded afternoon becomes the decisive moment of the battle. At F4, Shadow Recon's crews — still shaken from suppression — respond to Steel's pressure and begin pushing their vehicles off the highway into the farmland shoulder. Friction event 3 manifests here: a Steel Armor vehicle throws a track while maneuvering around Shadow's parked vehicles, creating a 20-minute delay as the crew works to clear the obstruction. Blue's high fortune (89) means Steel's column commander has anticipated exactly this kind of friction — bypass routes are pre-designated, and the column flows around the stalled vehicle with practiced efficiency. Shadow recovers from suppression during this period, its crews using the delay productively. Steel departs F4 approximately 30-40 minutes behind the ideal schedule but in good order, advancing through E4 toward D5. Shadow, now recovered, begins moving toward E4 to establish the forward observation post.

Simultaneously, Iron Fist executes its B6→C5 move. Red's fortune (71) means this is competent but unremarkable — Iron Fist arrives at Westbrook and begins taking hull-down positions among the buildings, coordinating sectors with Red Guard. The critical race is decided by timing: Iron Fist's 1-hex move through farmland is completed well within the 4-hour window, and given Steel's 30-40 minute delay at F4, Iron Fist is established at C5 before Steel reaches D5. Red Guard, though suppressed, begins recovering as Thunder's fires shift away from C5 toward C6. Friction event 2 (Red observes apparent Blue coordination failure) likely refers to the visible traffic jam at F4 observed by Viper Recon — this confirms to Red HQ that Iron Fist has time to establish before Blue arrives. Friction event 1 (Red sensor failures) degrades Viper's precision observation, but at 1km range in fog, engine noise and movement are still detectable.

Thunder Battery fires on C6 — but Iron Fist's route from B6 to C5 does not transit C6 (it is a direct 1-hex move). Thunder's rounds land on empty farmland at C6. Without a forward observer (Shadow is still recovering/moving to E4, not yet in position), Thunder cannot correct. The fires are wasted this turn. Hammer Battery, guided loosely by Viper's audio reports, fires on E5 and D5. The fog and sensor failures (friction 1) degrade accuracy significantly, but sustained area fire on the bridge approach creates a disruption zone. Alpha, advancing from F5 to E5, moves through the trailing edge of Hammer's beaten zone. The fires are fog-degraded and unobserved — insufficient to suppress a full-strength infantry battalion, but enough to disrupt its movement and coordination. Alpha's companies go to ground intermittently, lose formation cohesion, and arrive at E5 behind schedule and disorganized. Alpha is disrupted, not suppressed — the battalion retains its combat effectiveness and will to advance, but its coordination is degraded. Steel, pushing through E4 toward D5, enters the heavier portion of Hammer's suppression zone in the final approach and takes more direct fire effect, arriving at D5 suppressed with minor casualties. Steel finds Iron Fist established at C5 on the western bank. Per Blue's standing orders, Steel halts at D5 and calls for fire rather than assaulting a defended bridge against enemy armor. The bridge stands intact. The turn ends with Steel suppressed at D5, Alpha disrupted at E5, Iron Fist hull-down at C5, and a tense standoff across the Stonebrook.

### Turn 4
The afternoon battle reaches its climax under clear skies as dusk approaches. Thunder Battery opens its first properly observed fire mission of the campaign, with Shadow calling corrections from E4. The mission begins well — Shadow has genuine LOS to C5 and the first salvos bracket Westbrook. However, Blue's unfavorable fortune (31) manifests as a specific technical failure: Shadow's radio handset develops an intermittent fault under the vibration of nearby Steel's tank engines, causing correction transmissions to be garbled and requiring repeated retransmissions. The fire mission proceeds but with a 45-minute delay before corrections are fully effective. Thunder's fires on C5 achieve moderate suppression by mid-turn — Iron Fist's vehicles button up and Red Guard rotates to covered positions — but the buildings absorb significant fragmentation and neither unit is destroyed or routed. Iron Fist is suppressed but not degraded enough to trigger Steel's assault condition.

Simultaneously, Hammer Battery opens its maximum sustained fire mission on D5 and E5. Here, Red's radio net failure (friction event 1) proves decisive. Viper at C4 can observe the fall of shot perfectly in clear weather, but corrections must travel by runner through 5km of forest and jungle hills to reach Hammer at B9. The first correction cycle takes nearly an hour. Hammer fires with pre-registered data initially — accurate enough to maintain suppression on Steel at D5 and create a dangerous beaten zone, but not the precision observed fire Red planned. Alpha Company, advancing from E5 toward D5, moves through the degraded beaten zone. The fires are heavy but unguided — Alpha takes moderate casualties and is further disrupted, but the battalion pushes through and reaches D5 by mid-turn, joining Steel on the eastern bank. Alpha's arrival is costly but achieved. By the time Viper's first runner correction reaches Hammer, Alpha is already at D5 and Hammer shifts priority fires to D5 — but the window to stop Alpha in the open has passed.

At D5, the direct fire duel intensifies. Steel's tanks engage Iron Fist across the bridge at 1km — tank-on-tank fire at this range is lethal but Iron Fist is hull-down in urban cover. Both sides take attrition. Red Guard's AT teams engage Steel's exposed vehicles. Thunder's fires on C5 suppress Iron Fist enough to reduce its rate of fire but not eliminate it. Steel's assault condition — observable suppression of C5 — is not met by mid-turn. Steel's commander, per standing orders, holds at D5 and continues direct fire engagement rather than assaulting. The bridge remains uncrossed by armor this turn. Bravo's move to E6 is completed without incident — Sentinel holds at C7 and does not interfere. By dusk, the situation is: Steel and Alpha are consolidated at D5 under fire, C5 is suppressed but holding, and the bridge remains contested. Red's radio failure has cost it the precision fire advantage but its defensive position remains intact.
